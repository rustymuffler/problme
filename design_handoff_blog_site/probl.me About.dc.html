// about

Hi, I’m Richard.
I build in public.

I work in cybersecurity and SaaS product management, and on the side I tinker on personal projects with AI, writing down what I learn as I go. probl.me is that notebook, kept in the open.

Why this blog exists

I learn best by building the real thing and writing down what happened. Most posts about working with AI are either hype or fear. I wanted a third option: an honest log from someone actually shipping, including the parts that did not work.

So probl.me runs on the same principles I write about. The site is a static Astro build on GitHub Pages, the content moves through a pipeline of Claude agents, and every decision is documented. The process is the subject as much as the output.

What I’m building now

Two things, in parallel. probl.me is the blog you’re reading. Celly is a sister product I’m developing alongside it. Both are early, both are public, and both show up in what I write here.

If you’re a PM curious about AI, a developer figuring out how to work with agents, or someone non-technical who wants to manage their own software projects, I’m writing for you.
